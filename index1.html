The wetlands web-maps delivers an easy-to-use online map-like. It spatially integrates wetlands data with additional natural resource information and political boundaries to produce a robust decision support tool.

Wetlands play a pivotal role in the provision of ecological goods and services such as water, food and flood attenuation amongst other services. Zimbabwe is endowed with vast wetlands across the country which cover approximately 136 595.8 kmÂ² which is 34.96% of the total area of the country. Zimbabwe has wetlands of different types which are under threat from several factors such as climate change, land use change and population pressure. The Environmental Management Agency (EMA, 2016) observed that 21% of wetlands in Zimbabwe are severely degraded, 61% are moderately degraded while only 18% are regarded as stable or pristine.
